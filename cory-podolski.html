Cory Podolski, Dirt Track Racer and Car Designer Died Unexpectedly in A Sleep

August 15, 2024
post

Cory Podolski, commonly known as “CPod,” unexpectedly passed away in a terrible turn of circumstances, leaving the racing world in deep sorrow. On September 3, 2023, in Crandon, Cory, a former co-owner and vice president of Nine2zero Designs RC, died quietly in his sleep, leaving his loved ones, friends, and admirers in shock.

Cory Podolski, a native of Appleton, Wisconsin, a town perched above the Fox River and north of Lake Winnebago, was well-known for his prowess on the track and his amiable personality. He was adored in the tight-knit racing scene because of his outgoing nature; he was seen as a ray of happiness and optimism there.

Cory had a job at FASTSIGNS, a signage business specializing in custom signs and graphics products, in addition to his racing career. Beyond the racecourse, his love of design demonstrated his adaptability and originality.

Cory first made a name for himself in racing in 2001 when he competed in the Wisconsin Challenge Series and placed 65th. That year’s series champion was Mark Eswein. Cory participated in the ASA Late Model Series Challenge 2004 and earned a respectable 19th place. However, his performance dropped the following year, and he placed 78th in the same series.

Cory recently appeared on the registration list for the event at Crandon International Raceway on September 1-2. He planned to compete in the Pro Spec division, demonstrating his unwavering commitment to the sport. Before that, on August 12, 2023, at the Bark River International Raceway, he finished fifth, enhancing his standing as a skilled off-road racer.

Cory Podolski’s passing has devastated the racing world, and friends and teammates have gathered to pay tribute to his contagious humor, charming smiles, and unfailing support that impacted the lives of many.

The organization that oversees off-road racing, Championship Off-Road, paid tribute to Cory on their official Facebook page. They sent a poignant note to Cory’s family and friends, expressing their sorrow and praising his talent on the track and their friendliness. They invited people to share their treasured memories of Cory in the comments so that the racing industry may unite and honor his life.

Cori Lemke, a close friend and rival, expressed genuine gratitude for the wonderful times she shared with Cory on Facebook. Following his terrible passing, the racing community expressed gratitude for the chance to come to know Cory and for his profound influence on their lives. While the racing community mourns Cory Podolski’s loss, those who had the honor of knowing him will invariably cherish their memories of him.

The neighborhood will always treasure his unwavering support, outgoing personality, and love of racing. He impacted their lives, and they will always be grateful for that as they seek to uphold his legacy.

During this challenging time of mourning and reflection, we extend our heartfelt condolences to the family, friends, and supporters of Cory Podolski. The racing community will deeply mourn his loss, and those lucky enough to have known him will forever cherish their shared memories. Our thoughts and prayers are with everyone affected by this tragedy.
