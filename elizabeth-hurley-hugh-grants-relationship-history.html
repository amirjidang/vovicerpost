Inside Elizabeth Hurley And Hugh Grant's Relationship

Elizabeth Hurley and Hugh Grant had quite a dramatic relationship that is fascinating to look back on. They dated for 13 years from 1987 to 2000. According to Us Weekly, they went through a huge scandal in 1995 when Grant's affair with Divine Brown, a sex worker, became public. This was a very intense experience for both Hurley and Grant... and since they didn't break up right away, the rest of their relationship must have felt intense as well.

As The Guardian reported, Grant was arrested when seen with Brown in his car. After being told that he had to attend a program about AIDs and pay $1,000, he said that he and Hurley were going to stay together. He said, "I’ve done an abominable thing, and she’s [Liz Hurley] been amazing about it, and contrary to what I read in the paper today, she’s been very supportive, and we’re going to try to work it out.’’

Both actors have shared their perspectives on their breakup over the years and have opened up quite a lot to Andy Cohen. According to Elle, the stars both talked about their relationship on Watch What Happens Live with Andy Cohen in 2015.

Hugh Grant seemed to suggest that the relationship became less romantic as time went on. He said:

"The sex bit probably fizzled out, but now she's my absolute best friend—number one person I call in a crisis."

Are Elizabeth Hurley And Hugh Grant Friends Today?

In 2021, Elizabeth Hurley was interviewed on the podcast People In The '90s and said that she and Hugh Grant remained friends after breaking up. According to USA Today, she believes that if people want to stay on good terms with a former partner, they should "have a lot of respect for their current life."

She said:

"We haven't been romantically involved for 20 years… But we're always very aware that there are other people in our lives. There's partners, there's children. You can't just, sort of, live in a lovely, rosy, mist of the past."
