Singer-songwriter Mickey Guyton is the second oldest child of her parents and is of Black ethnicity.

Mickey Guyton is a 38-year-old singer and songwriter who is prominently known for her 2020 single, Black Like Me. The song is about her experience as a Black woman and was released during the global Black Lives Matter movement.

Guyton debuted with the Capitol Records Nashville in 2015 with her debut extended play Unbreakable. She also released her debut single in the same year titled Better Than You Left Me.

Mickey Guyton Parents, Nationality & Ethnicity

Mickey Guyton’s parents, Phyllis Ann Roddy and Michael Eugene Guyton are of black ethnicity.

Guyton was born in Arlington, Texas, and holds American nationality. Her father was an engineer and they had to move around a lot during her childhood due to her father’s job.

She and her three siblings initially attended a local public school. But they later switched to a private school after facing racial discrimination from other families in the neighborhood.

Meet Mickey Guyton Husband Grant Savoy

Mickey Guyton and her husband Grant Savoy got engaged in 2016 after dating for six years.

They finally tied the knot in 2017 in an intimate wedding ceremony in Kauai, Hawaii, in the presence of their friends and families.

Guyton announced her pregnancy in August 2020 and delivered a healthy boy a few months later in January 2021. The couple named their son Grayson.

Furthermore, her husband, Grant is a lawyer from Los Angeles, California. He is a graduate of California State University, Northridge, as per Heavy.

He has worked in both government and private sectors such as the Los Angeles County District Attorney’s Office and the Office of the Ventura County Public Defender.
