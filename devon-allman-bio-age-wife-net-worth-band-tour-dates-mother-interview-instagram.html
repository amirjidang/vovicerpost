Devon Allman Band

He headed up a band in St. Louis called The Dark Horses in the early 1990s. In 1999, he formed Honeytribe. The band, however, broke up in 2001 so that he could spend time with his newly born son.

In 2005, they re-formed with their original lineup, and have since toured in the United States, Europe, and Canada, and have recorded two albums. In 2006, they recorded Torch and played up to 300 shows a year in 42 states and ten countries. He eventually grew restless with the original Honeytribe sound and pared the band down to a power trio in 2008 with bassist George Potsos and new drummer Gabriel Strange. This incarnation of Devon’s Honeytribe released Space Age Blues in the fall of 2010.

He began working on a project titled the Royal Southern Brotherhood in October 2011. The blues-rock supergroup includes Mike Zito, Grammy-award-winning drummer Yonrico Scott, bassist Charlie Wooton, and Cyril Neville.

The sons of Gregg Allman and Dickey Betts announced the formation of The Allman Betts Band in November 2018. They will kick off 2019 with a new Allman Betts Band album and a worldwide tour that will feature new music, songs from their solo projects, and classic Allman Brothers and Gregg Allman tunes in honor of the 50th Anniversary of The Allman Brothers Band. The new album, which is slated to be released in the Spring of 2019, will be produced by Matt Ross-Spang (Jason Isbell, Margo Price, John Prine, and Elvis Presley, with no relation to the musician of the same name).

Devon finished recording sessions for his first solo album, titled Turquoise in September 2012 which features Myles Weeks on bass and Yonrico Scott on drums. Those sessions were recorded at Ardent Studios, in Memphis, Tennessee, engineered by Pete Matthews and produced by Jim Gaines, and include guest musicians Luther Dickinson, Samantha Fish, Rick Steff, and Ron Holloway. In November 2012, a solo tour began and the touring band includes Pedro Arevalo on bass, Bobby Schneck Jr. on the second guitar, and Anthony Nanney on drums. On 12 February 2013, the album was released.

In 2005, he appeared on Love, Union, Peace with bluesman Javier Vargas’ Vargas Blues Band (along with Jack Bruce, Reese Wynans, and others), and in 2008 on the Flamenco Blues Experience recording by the same outfit. Devon guested on Paris Luna’s City Lights album in 2007. Also, he has made appearances at various festivals and on MTV Europe.

Devon Allman Achievements

Devon was the founder and bandleader of Honeytribe, also known as Devon Allman’s Honeytribe, with whom he released two albums and toured across North America and Europe. Before Honeytribe, he contributed to several other musical recordings, notably Vargas Blues Band and the A Song for My Father compilation album.

Allman was one of the original members of the Royal Southern Brotherhood and contributed to their first two studio albums and toured with them. In 2013, he launched his solo career as the Devon Allman Band and has since released three albums. His latest tour, branded as the Devon Allman Project, features special guest Duane Betts.

The Devon Allman Project

The project is a six-piece band featuring two percussionists, John Lum & R. Scott Bryan (Sheryl Crow), bassist Justin Corgan, guitarist Jackson Stokes and a Hammond B3 player, Nicholas David. The club/theater shows feature a 30-minute opening set with Duane Betts using his go-to guitar player, Johnny Stachel, and Devon’s rhythm section.

After a 15 minute break, the Devon Allman Project plays a one-hour set which will include songs from Honeytribe, Royal Southern Brotherhood, The Devon Allman Band, and a few covers. The night culminates with a 30 minute plus encore featuring all eight musicians playing Allman Brothers tunes and other classics.

Devon Allman Music

Devon began playing music as a teenager but was not influenced by his father. He did not meet his father until he was 17 years old but they then bonded instantly. For several years in the 1990s, Allman performed around the growing St. Louis Blues and rock music scene, while also managing a suburban Guitar Center store where he met his future Royal Southern Brotherhood bandmate, Mike Zito.

During his twenties, he tried various musical styles and sounds to distance himself from his father’s sound and avoid obvious comparisons, though his father did not meddle in his career. Devon embraced the blues and rock genres during his thirties.

He grew up on classic, blues-inspired rock music, and has specifically mentioned Santana, The Rolling Stones, The Doors, and The Allman Brothers Band as influences, as well as heavy metal acts such as Iron Maiden, Metallica, Megadeth, and Testament.
