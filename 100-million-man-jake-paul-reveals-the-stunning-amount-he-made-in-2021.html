$100 Million Man: Jake Paul Reveals The Stunning Amount He Made In 2021

August 17, 2024
post

Jake Paul recently opened up about the staggering amount of wealth he banked last year, through boxing and other ventures. The Youtuber-turned-boxer claims that he made $40 million through boxing alone, and another $60 million through investing and business ventures which helped the 25-year-old rake in roughly $100 million last year.

In an interview with Fox Business, Paul explained that he had earned $40 million from his successful foray into boxing. The Youtuber defeated Ben Askren in March with a TKO in the first round, earning millions from a fight that lasted less than 2 minutes. He followed the successful bout by defeating Tyron Woodley twice, later in the year.

The boxer received a purse just for showing up to the fight, but most of his profit was generated from his share of the pay-per-view purchases.

"That is just what I made from boxing," Paul said of the $40 million he made in the ring. "I made some more on top of that from brand deals and advertisements.”

But it was his other business ventures that helped the rising star become a centimillionaire.

"That is not including my crypto investments, NFT investments, Metaverse investments. If I were to cash out, yeah I have probably made an extra $60 million on top of that just from my investments last year if I were to liquidate it.”

Paul also says that he invested his money in bitcoin when he was sixteen years old.

Paul told Fox Business that he liked “the challenge of making money” and the challenge of “accomplishing something” that he didn’t think was possible. When asked if he could make $100 million again in 2022, he said he was “working on it.”

"I put money into Bitcoin when I was sixteen years old but I am a long-term holder so I can add to the retirement fund. I have my team at Morgan Stanley and they handle all this stuff,” he continued.

Paul recently appeared on his brother Logan Paul’s podcast, Impaulsive, and boasted that he had earned more than the entire UFC roster combined. The boxer has been using his platform to challenge UFC president Dana White over fighter pay.
