Lori Lightfoot is an American politician, attorney, and the current mayor of Chicago, Illinois. She was born on August 4, 1962, in Massillon, Ohio.

Before becoming the mayor of Chicago, Lightfoot served as a federal prosecutor in the Northern District of Illinois and later worked as a partner at the law firm of Mayer Brown.

Lightfoot was elected as the 56th mayor of Chicago in April 2019, becoming both the first African American woman and the first openly gay person to hold the office.

In addition to her work as mayor, Lightfoot has been involved in a number of community organizations and has served on various boards and commissions, including the Chicago Police Memorial Foundation and the Illinois State Criminal Justice Information Authority.

Lori Lightfoot Approval Rating

When Mayor Lori Lightfoot ran for the city’s top job in 2019, she did so as an outsider, garnering 70 per cent of the vote in a runoff election in which she pledged to ferret out corruption in City Hall.

Lori Lightfoot Before Mayor

After law school, Lightfoot became a practising attorney at the Mayer Brown law firm, serving a wide cross-section of clients. Lightfoot first entered the public sector as Assistant United States Attorney for the Northern District of Illinois.

Lori Lightfoot Young

As a child, Lori Lightfoot grew up in a working-class family in Ohio. Her mother, Ann Lightfoot, was a nighttime healthcare aide and school board member, and her father, Elijah Lightfoot, was a local factory worker and janitor.

Lori Lightfoot Education

She graduated from high school in 1980 and went on to attend the University of Michigan, where she earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Political Science in 1984. She later earned her Juris Doctor degree from the University of Chicago Law School in 1989.
