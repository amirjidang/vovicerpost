Bath & Body Works, Inc. is an American specialty retail company based in Columbus, Ohio. It was founded in 1963 by Les Wexner and is currently owned by L Brands, Inc. Bath & Body Works is the largest specialty retail beauty brand globally, with over $11.8 billion in revenue in 2021. The company operates over 1,600 stores in North America and has an additional 85 stores internationally. Bath & Body Works is known for its wide range of products, including body lotions, fragrances, soaps, and candles.

The History of Bath and Body Works

Founded in 1963 by Les Wexner, Bath & Body Works began its journey as a small store in Upper Arlington, Ohio. Les Wexner borrowed $5,000 from his aunt and $5,000 from the bank to open the store at the Kingsdale Shopping Center. Originally named “The Limited,” the store initially focused on clothing for younger women.

In 1990, Bath & Body Works underwent a repositioning and transformed into a beauty line for Express, Inc. With this change, the company changed its name to Bath & Body Works, paving the way for its expansion and success. Since then, Bath & Body Works has evolved, diversifying its product offerings and becoming the largest specialty retail beauty brand globally.

Today, Bath & Body Works is known for its extensive range of products, including body lotions, fragrances, soaps, and candles. With over 1,600 stores in North America and additional stores internationally, the company continues to grow and captivate customers with its exceptional quality and variety.

Ownership Changes and Spin-off

Bath & Body Works, a renowned beauty and fragrance brand, has experienced various ownership changes throughout its rich history. In a significant move announced in 2021, L Brands, Inc., the parent company, decided to spin off Bath & Body Works as a stand-alone publicly traded company, allowing it to operate independently.

The spin-off process was successfully completed in August 2021, marking a new chapter for Bath & Body Works. As a result, the company began trading on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol “BBWI.” This strategic decision was made to unlock the full potential of Bath & Body Works and enhance its ability to focus on its core retail operations.

Prior to the spin-off, Bath & Body Works was part of L Brands, Inc., which also owned another well-known brand, Victoria’s Secret. However, the planned majority stake sale of Victoria’s Secret to Sycamore Partners fell through, leading to a separate course of action for Bath & Body Works.
